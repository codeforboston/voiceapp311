Reopening during COVID-19 in Boston

The City of Boston is in Step 1 of Phase 3 of the state's reopening plan. Boston City Hall is open to the public on Tuesdays, Thursdays, and Fridays from 9 a.m. - 5 p.m. We continue to update City-specific guidance on our reopening website.

Back to top
Tuesday, August 11
Status of City departments

City Hall is only open to the public on Tuesdays, Thursdays, and Fridays from 9 a.m. - 5 p.m. If you need to visit City Hall, you must make an appointment.
Meal sites for youth

We're providing free breakfast and lunch, Monday through Friday, across the City.
COVID-19 testing sites

Testing is free, regardless of insurance or immigration status. Please call ahead before visiting a location.
Community centers

Boston Centers for Youth and Families is offering a menu of free programs and classes for Boston youth this summer, both remote and in-person.
Libraries

Boston Public Library locations are closed, but you can order and pick up physical items through the new BPL to Go program.
Parking meters

Residents with a valid resident parking permit sticker can park in a metered or two-hour parking space in their neighborhood without having to adhere to the time limit, or pay a meter fee.
Trash and recycling

Pickup is on a normal schedule.
Street Cleaning

Street cleaning is on a normal schedule. However, we are not ticketing or towing for street sweeping at this time. Ticketing will resume on Monday, August 10, 2020.
Restaurants and bars

Starting on Monday, June 22, seated indoor dining is allowed at restaurants in Boston with certain conditions.
Tow lot

The tow lot is open Monday through Friday, 7 a.m. - 11 p.m. Automated kiosks are available 24 hours a day, seven days a week for vehicle releases.
