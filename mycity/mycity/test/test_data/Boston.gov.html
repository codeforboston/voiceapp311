Coronavirus (COVID-19) updates

All K-12 schools in Massachusetts and non-emergency childcare programs are closed through the end of the school year. Strict measures are still in place in Boston, including a Public Health Advisory for everyone except essential workers to stay at home from 9 p.m. to 6 a.m. each day.

Back to top
Saturday, April 25
Schools closed

Governor Baker announced the closure of K-12 schools in Massachusetts and non-emergency childcare programs through the end of the school year. 
City building hours

City Hall will only be open to the public on Tuesdays and Fridays, from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. All library locations and BCYF community centers are closed.
Status of City departments

Many departments have closed their offices to the public. If you need to visit City Hall for essential services, you must make an appointment.
Trash and recycling

Due to the holiday, pickups are delayed in some neighborhoods this week. Please check your schedule.
Street Cleaning

Street cleaning is on a normal schedule. However, we are not ticketing or towing for street sweeping at this time.
Parking meters

Residents with a valid resident parking permit sticker can park in a metered or two-hour parking space in their neighborhood without having to adhere to the time limit, or pay a meter fee.
Tow lot

The tow lot is open Monday through Friday, 9:30 a.m. - 4:30 p.m. Automated kiosks are available 24 hours a day, seven days a week for vehicle releases.
Meal sites for youth

With Boston Public Schools closed, the City is providing free breakfast and lunch meals, Monday through Friday, across the City.
Restaurants and bars

Governor Baker issued an emergency order prohibiting on-premises consumption of food or drink at bars and restaurants.
City construction

Mayor Walsh has suspended all non-essential construction for City of Boston permitted sites.
